Our Home

Welcome to Sand Point, Alaska

Nestled on Popof Island along the Alaska Peninsula, Sand Point is a vibrant fishing community with a rich cultural heritage and breathtaking natural beauty. With approximately 950 residents, the town offers the warmth of a small community where neighbors know each other by name.

Sand Point serves as the commercial hub of the Aleutians East Borough, home to one of Alaska's most productive fisheries. Life here means waking up to stunning mountain views, fresh ocean air, and the kind of authentic community connection that is increasingly rare.

For educators who value purpose-driven work and a life beyond the ordinary, Sand Point is a place that will challenge, inspire, and reward you in equal measure.

Life in Sand Point

Everything You Need to Know

Moving to a remote Alaskan community is a big step. Here's an honest look at what daily life looks like.

✈️ Transportation

Sand Point is accessible by small aircraft via Peninsula Airways (PenAir) from Anchorage or King Salmon. Daily scheduled flights connect residents to the wider world. A state ferry (Alaska Marine Highway) provides seasonal service. In town, most residents travel by car, ATV, or on foot — the community is compact and walkable.

🏡 Housing

Rental housing is available in Sand Point, and the district assists new hires in locating accommodations. Rent is generally lower than Anchorage or other urban centers. Some district-provided housing may be available for qualifying staff. Property ownership opportunities exist for those planning longer-term stays.

🛒 Groceries & Shopping

Sand Point has a local general store for everyday groceries and household items. Costs are higher than urban areas due to freight shipping, so many residents supplement with bulk orders or barge deliveries. Fresh seafood is plentiful and affordable locally. Most residents plan supply runs to Anchorage 1–2 times per year.

🌐 Internet & Connectivity

High-speed internet service is available in Sand Point, supporting both work-from-home and personal use. Cell phone service is provided by GCI and similar Alaska providers. Connectivity has improved significantly in recent years and supports streaming, video calls, and remote work.

🏥 Healthcare

Sand Point has a local clinic that handles routine and urgent care needs, staffed by medical professionals through the Eastern Aleutian Tribes health system. For major procedures or specialized care, residents travel to King Salmon or Anchorage. Emergency medical services and medevac flights are available when needed.

🎭 Cultural Activities

Sand Point has a rich Aleut (Unangan) cultural heritage. Community events, potlucks, and celebrations are central to life here. The school hosts performances, sports events, and community gatherings. Local history and indigenous traditions are woven into daily life, offering educators a unique cultural learning experience.

🎣 Outdoor Recreation

Sand Point is an outdoor enthusiast's dream. World-class halibut and salmon fishing, kayaking, wildlife viewing (eagles, bears, marine mammals), hiking, and beachcombing are all on your doorstep. Winter brings snowshoeing and a dramatic, moody landscape unlike anywhere else.

⚠️ Important Considerations

Alaska is a permanent fund dividend state — non-residents may have limitations on PFD eligibility their first year. Weather can be unpredictable and flights may be delayed due to fog or wind. Social life centers around the community itself rather than restaurants or nightlife. This is a remote posting — embracing that is key to loving it.
